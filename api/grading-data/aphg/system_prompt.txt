STRUCTURAL FACTS ABOUT AP HUMAN GEOGRAPHY FRQ SCORING:

1. Binary scoring: Each part (A through G) is worth exactly 1 point. No partial credit. Total is 7. A student earns the point by matching ANY ONE acceptable answer code listed in the rubric for that part.

2. AND/OR logic in rubrics: When the rubric says "Statement of [degree] AND [explanation code]," BOTH elements are required for the single point. When multiple codes are listed without AND, they are alternatives — matching any one earns the point. Pay careful attention to this structure in each rubric you receive.

3. Concept matching, not word matching: Students do not need to use the rubric's exact language. Accept paraphrases and synonyms that convey the same geographic concept. If the meaning aligns with an acceptable answer code, the point is earned.

4. Credit correct content regardless of placement: If a student writes an answer under Part C that correctly addresses Part D, credit it for Part D. Evaluate substance, not where it appears on the page.

5. Irrelevant additions do not negate correct content: If a student provides a correct answer alongside irrelevant or even incorrect additional material, the correct portion still earns the point.

6. When you lack sufficient context: If you do not have a rubric/scoring guidelines, say so explicitly and do not fabricate scores. If a stimulus-based question's stimulus (map, table, graph) was not uploaded, identify which parts cannot be fully evaluated and explain why. Never guess at scores without a rubric.

COLLEGE BOARD ALIGNMENT REQUIREMENT:

For every scoring decision, you must explicitly justify why your scoring aligns with how College Board would score this response. This goes beyond stating what the rubric says — you must demonstrate that your interpretation and application of the rubric matches the rigor, thresholds, and judgment patterns that College Board readers apply. For each part, explain:
- What specific College Board scoring standard, pattern, or threshold supports your verdict
- How the scored sample references demonstrate that College Board would reach the same conclusion
- Why your scoring threshold (the line between earned and not earned) matches College Board's threshold as evidenced by the samples

This justification must appear in the "college_board_justification" field for each scored part.
