General Scoring Notes  
• 
Each point is earned independently. 
• 
Accuracy: These scoring guidelines require that students demonstrate historically defensible content 
knowledge. Given the timed nature of the exam, responses may contain errors that do not detract from 
their overall quality, as long as the historical content used to advance the argument is accurate. 
• 
Clarity: Exam responses should be considered first drafts and thus may contain grammatical errors. 
Those errors will not be counted against a student unless they obscure the successful demonstration of 
the content knowledge, skills, and practices described below. 
• 
Describe: Provide the relevant characteristics of a specified topic. Description requires more than simply 
mentioning an isolated term. 
• 
Explain: Provide information about how or why a historical development or process occurs or how or 
why a relationship exists.   
 
(A) 
Identify ONE technological development that contributed to Europeans’ ability to spread 
religious ideas in the Americas during the period 1450 to 1750. 
Examples that earn this point include the following:  
• 
The development of new ships, such as the caravel, contributed to Europeans’ ability 
to spread religious ideas in the Americas during the period 1450 to 1750. 
• 
The printing press is a technological development that helped spread European 
religious ideas. 
• 
Improved cartography and transoceanic travel contributed to Europeans’ ability to 
spread religious ideas in the Americas. 
1 point 
 
 
(B) 
Explain ONE way that religion contributed to the process of state expansion and/or 
exploration in the Americas during the period 1450 to 1750.

European missionaries often supported colonialism in order to spread Christianity.  
• 
Spanish monarchs and conquistadors used religion to justify their colonization of the 
Americas.   
• 
The creation of new religious orders, such as the Jesuits, contributed to Catholic 
attempts to control and enforce religious orthodoxy in Spanish and Portuguese 
colonies in the Americas. 
 
1 point

Short Answer Question 3 
Note: Student samples are quoted verbatim and may contain spelling and grammatical errors. 
Overview 
 
The intent of this question was for students to address the spread of religion in the Americas from 
1450–1750. Part A asked students to identify one technological development that contributed to 
Europeans’ ability to spread religious ideas in the Americas during the period 1450–1750. Part B 
asked students to explain one way that religion contributed to the process of state expansion and/or 
exploration in the Americas during the period 1450–1750. Part C asked students to explain one 
reason why syncretic belief systems developed in the Americas during the period 1450–1750. 
 
The question was primarily focused on the reasoning process of causation. 
 
The question tested content primarily from Topics 4.1, 4.4, and 4.5 of the course framework. 
Sample: 3A 
Score: 3 
 
a) The response earned 1 point for identifying the “caravel ship” as a technological development that 
contributed to the spread of religious ideas in the Americas. 
 
b) The response earned 1 point for explaining that “monumental architecture related to Christianity” 
contributed to the process of state expansion in the Americas.  
 
c) The response earned 1 point for explaining the reason that syncretic belief systems developed in 
the Americas by stating that Indigenous populations “clung on to their personal beliefs and 
traditions.” 
Sample: 3B 
Score: 2 
 
a) The response earned 1 point for identifying “maritime technology like advanced boats and 
compass” as technological developments that contributed to the spread of religious ideas in the 
Americas. 
 
b) The response earned 1 point for explaining that “many scholars and missionairie were sent to 
explore to spread their religion,” which contributed to the process of state expansion. 
 
c) The response did not earn the point for explaining a reason for the development of syncretic 
beliefs. “Missionaires/scholars would bump into each other sharing their ideas, language, culture 
and religion” does not discuss the merging, or creation, of belief systems among the various groups 
of people in the Americas.
