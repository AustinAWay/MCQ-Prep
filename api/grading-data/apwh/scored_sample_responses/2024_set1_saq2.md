Question 2: Short Answer Primary Source 
3 points 
General Scoring Notes 
• 
Each point is earned independently. 
• 
Accuracy: These scoring guidelines require that students demonstrate historically defensible content 
knowledge. Given the timed nature of the exam, responses may contain errors that do not detract from 
their overall quality, as long as the historical content used to advance the argument is accurate. 
• 
Clarity: Exam responses should be considered first drafts and thus may contain grammatical errors. 
Those errors will not be counted against a student unless they obscure the successful demonstration of 
the content knowledge, skills, and practices described below. 
• 
Describe: Provide the relevant characteristics of a specified topic. Description requires more than 
simply mentioning an isolated term. 
• 
Explain: Provide information about how or why a historical development or process occurs or how or 
why a relationship exists. 
[a] 
Identify ONE likely political purpose of the image. 
1 point 
Examples that earn this point include the following: 
• 
The poster was created to inspire people to vote for the Nazi party. 
• 
The poster was created to convince people that voting for the Nazi party would 
solve their economic problems. 
• 
The poster was intended to make people angry with the current government and its 
management of the economy. 
[b] 
Explain ONE way the image illustrates the economic situation of the period after the 
First World War. 
1 point 
Examples that earn this point include the following: 
• 
The image shows the poverty that many families in Germany and elsewhere faced 
because of the Great Depression. 
• 
The image illustrates the growing popularity of the idea that governments should 
take a more active role in economic life. 
• 
The image shows that Germany suffered greatly following its defeat in the First 
World War, including unemployment, reparations, or inflation.

Short Answer Question 2 
Note: Student samples are quoted verbatim and may contain spelling and grammatical errors. 
Overview 
Students were expected to answer three questions based on a primary-source image:  an election 
poster from 1932 urging people to vote for the German National Socialist Party. For part (a), students 
were asked to identify a likely political purpose of the image. For part (b), students were asked to 
explain a way that the image illustrated the economic situation of the period after the First World 
War. For part (c), students were asked to explain a way that the rise of the German National Socialist 
Party led to the Second World War.  
The question tested content primarily from Unit 7 of the course framework, specifically Topics 7.4, 
7.5, and 7.6. Students could also leverage knowledge from Topics 7.2, 7.3, and 7.7 to better develop 
their responses. 
Sample: 2A 
Score: 3  
The response earned the point for part (a) because it correctly identifies a purpose of the poster as 
“to instill a dislike among the citizens for the current government” and claims Hitler will help save 
the citizens from economic despair.  
The response earned the point for part (b) because it correctly explains how the poster illustrates the 
connection between German war reparations and the economic depression in Germany after the 
First World War.  
The response earned the point for part (c) because it correctly explains the connection between 
rising militarism under the Nazis with the invasion of Poland. In addition, the response correctly 
explains the post-WWI desire for vengeance based upon Germany’s economic depression.  
Sample: 2B 
Score: 2  
The response earned the point for part (a) when it correctly identifies the poster’s purpose as 
“gaining a vote for Hitler.” 
The response earned the point for part (b) when it explains that after the First World War, Germany’s 
debt crippled their economy. 
The response did not earn the point for part (c) because the statement that the Nazis were “so angry 
for taking all of the blame from the First World War” identifies one of the key pieces of the Nazi 
Party’s platform, but it does not explain how the rise of the Nazi Party led to the Second World War.

Sample: 2C 
Score: 1  
The response earned the point for part (a) because it correctly identifies the purpose of the poster as 
encouraging people to “vote for Adolf Hitler.”  
The response did not earn the point for part (b) because “adults were jobless” is a restatement of 
what is in the caption. To earn the point, the response must go beyond what is in the caption to 
explain how the image reflects the economic situation after World War I.  
The response did not earn the point for part (c) because it does not explain how “creating 
inequalities” led to the Second World War.
