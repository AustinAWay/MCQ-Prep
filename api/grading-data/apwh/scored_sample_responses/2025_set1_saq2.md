AP® World History: Modern 2025 Scoring Guidelines 
Question 2: Short Answer Primary Source 
3 points 
General Scoring Notes 
• 
Each point is earned independently. 
• 
Accuracy: These scoring guidelines require that students demonstrate historically defensible content 
knowledge. Given the timed nature of the exam, responses may contain errors that do not detract from 
their overall quality, as long as the historical content used to advance the argument is accurate. 
• 
Clarity: Exam responses should be considered first drafts and thus may contain grammatical errors. 
Those errors will not be counted against a student unless they obscure the successful demonstration of 
the content knowledge, skills, and practices described below. 
• 
Describe: Provide the relevant characteristics of a specified topic. Description requires more than 
simply mentioning an isolated term. 
• 
Explain: Provide information about how or why a historical development or process occurs or how or 
why a relationship exists. 
A 
Identify one likely audience for the claims that the author makes in the passage. 
1 point 
Examples of acceptable responses may include the following: 
• 
One likely audience is women. 
• 
One likely audience is middle-class people of both genders. 
• 
One likely audience could be both women and men who share her feminist ideas 
about the importance of promoting education and using the press. 
B 
Describe one historical context during the nineteenth century that explains the 
increased poverty and misery referred to in the fourth paragraph. 
1 point 
Examples of acceptable responses may include the following: 
• 
Rapid urbanization during the nineteenth century led to increases in poverty among 
members of the working class. 
• 
As more workers moved to cities during the Industrial Revolution period, numbers 
grew to such an extent that there was extreme overcrowding. 
• 
During industrialization factory owners had a lot of power and workers did not have 
the ability to negotiate for better wages or better working conditions, which 
resulted in widespread poverty among workers. 
C 
Explain how one ideology or set of ideas likely influenced the author’s claims in the 
passage. 
1 point 
Examples of acceptable responses may include the following: 
• 
Feminism is an ideology that probably influenced the author because it emphasized 
the importance of education for women, which is consistent with her publishing the 
newspaper to uplift the condition of women.

Total Score: 3
The response earned 1 point for Part A because it identifies “women in Germany” as a likely audience 
for the claims made in the passage. Even though this would have been sufficient to earn the point for an 
identification task, the response also provides outside evidence to explain why women would have been 
a likely audience, discussing the early feminist movement, Mary Wollstonecraft, Olympe de Gouges, etc.
The response earned 1 point for Part B because it provides a detailed explanation of how the Industrial 
Revolution is a likely historical context that accounts for the increased poverty and misery of the 
industrial working classes, as described in the passage. The response brings up multiple aspects of 
the factory system that increased workers’ misery and/or poverty, discussing extreme specialization 
of labor, unsanitary working conditions, long work hours, and low wages. The response also correctly 
describes the migration of industrial workers from the countryside to the cities, and the resulting 
“squalid conditions in housing spaces like tenements.” The response’s description in Part B is much 
more detailed than the minimum that would have been needed to earn the point.
The response earned 1 point for Part C because it explains how “enlightenment ideas of natural rights 
produced by John Locke” likely influenced the writer’s claims. The response also explains that the 
author’s use of “indivudalistic ideas” was also likely influenced by the Enlightenment although this part 
of the response was less clear.

Short Answer Question 2 (continued)
The response earned 1 point for Part B because it describes how the Industrial Revolution increased 
workers’ poverty and misery by causing families “to migrate to other parts of the world and work to 
obtain money.” Although this description of a historical context is overly generalized it was deemed 
accurate enough to earn the point since industrialization did lead to an increase in international and 
interregional migration.
The response did not earn the point for Part C. The response’s statement that “One ideology or set of 
ideas likely influenced the author’s claims in the passage by setting an example” does not identify an 
ideology or a set of ideas that influenced the author’s arguments in the passage.
